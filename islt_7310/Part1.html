Part 1: Spicy pepper/chili oil


Ingredients


  • 50g Qixing peppers (very spicy)
  • 350 g Zidantou red peppers (bright in color, not very spicy)
  • 350 g Zidantou red peppers (bright in color, not very spicy)
  • 450g Dried Lantern peppers (aromatic, not very spicy)
  • 3000g Canola oil
  • 300g ginger sliced, skin-on
  • 100g green onion, coarsely chopped
  • 150g shallot julienned
  • 15g star anise
  • 20g cinnamon sticks
  • 10g sani
  • 2 each black cardamom, smashed
  • 3g bay leave
  • 20g stir-fry bullet head peppers, dried, sliced in inch pieces
  • 50g sesame seeds

Directions


  1. Lightly roast and grind peppers. half coarse, half fine.
  2. Heat canola oil until smoking to remove any raw flavors (about 475F or 80%).
    Remove from heat and allow to cool to 50% (about 300F) (maybe 30%, 200F)
  3. Place wok back over low heat. Add ginger, green onion, big green onions, shallots,
    star anise, cinnamon, Sani, cardamom, and bay leaves. Cook until onions are golden brown (about 15 minutes).
  4. Remove all ingredients from oil and discard. Heat to 60% (350F).
  5. Combine coarse ground peppers and sesame seeds in a large bowl. Add enough hot oil to cover peppers.
    Add fine ground peppers and remaining oil. Cool and use as needed.
